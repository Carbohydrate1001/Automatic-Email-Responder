📊 Rubric-Based Scoring System

1. Classification Rubric

Dimension Weight Score Label Criteria
keyword_match
Keyword presence		 25% 0 No Match No relevant keywords found
1 Generic Match Generic business keywords only
2 Partial Match 1-2 category-specific keywords
3 Strong Match 3+ category-specific keywords, clearly indicate category
intent_clarity
Customer intent clarity		 30% 0 Unclear Intent unclear or ambiguous, multiple interpretations
1 Somewhat Clear General intent identifiable, lacks specific details
2 Clear Intent clearly stated, minor details missing
3 Crystal Clear Intent explicitly stated, all relevant details provided, no ambiguity
context_completeness
Sufficient context		 25% 0 Missing Context Essential information absent, cannot categorize confidently
1 Minimal Context Basic information present, missing supporting details
2 Adequate Context Sufficient information to categorize, some supporting details
3 Rich Context Comprehensive information, multiple supporting details, background context
exclusion_confidence
Exclusion from other categories		 20% 0 Multiple Categories Could belong to 3+ categories, no clear primary
1 Some Overlap Matches 2 categories, primary identifiable but uncertain
2 Minimal Overlap Clearly belongs to one category, minor overlap with 1 other
3 Clearly Distinct Uniquely matches one category, no overlap, unambiguous
Formula:
weighted_score = Σ(dimension_score × weight)
confidence = weighted_score / 3.0
Range: 0.0 - 1.0

2. Auto-Send Rubric

Dimension Weight Score Label Criteria
information_completeness
Reply quality & completeness		 30% 0 Cannot Respond Customer intent completely unclear, cannot generate meaningful response
1 Generic Only Can acknowledge receipt but cannot address specific need
2 Appropriate Response Addresses category/intent, clearly explains missing info, provides specific guidance
3 Complete & Actionable Complete, clear, professional reply. Either directly resolves OR provides standard request-info template
risk_level
Risk of incorrect response		 35% 0 High Risk Financial commitment, legal implications, safety concerns, requires human judgment
1 Medium Risk Could significantly impact customer satisfaction, moderate financial implications
2 Low Risk Standard inquiry type, minimal financial impact, low error probability
3 Minimal Risk Routine inquiry, no financial implications, standard template perfect fit. Requesting missing info is inherently low-risk
template_applicability
Template fit for scenario		 20% 0 Doesn't Apply Unique situation not covered by template, needs custom response
1 Partial Fit Template covers general case, significant modifications needed, multiple placeholders
2 Good Fit Template covers this scenario, minor customization needed, few placeholders
3 Perfect Fit Template designed for this exact case. Standard "request information" templates apply perfectly
policy_alignment
Company policy compliance		 15% 0 Potential Violation May violate company policy, commitment beyond authority
1 Uncertain Policy applicability unclear, edge case not covered
2 Likely Compliant Follows general policy guidelines, no obvious violations
3 Clearly Compliant Explicitly follows policy, pre-approved response type
Auto-Send Decision Thresholds:
✓ auto_send_minimum: ≥ 2.5 / 3.0
✓ require_all_above: > 1 (no dimension = 0)
✓ high_confidence: ≥ 2.7 / 3.0
Formula:
weighted_score = Σ(dimension_score × weight)
confidence = weighted_score / 3.0
auto_send = (weighted_score ≥ 2.5) AND (all dimensions > 1)
Key Insight: "Requesting missing information" is NOT a failure case - when intent is clear, it's a complete, professional, low-risk customer service response that should be auto-sent. The system now correctly distinguishes: ❌ Cannot understand customer intent → Manual review | ✅ Clear intent + missing info → Auto-send "please provide X" | ✅ Clear intent + all info present → Auto-send resolution
